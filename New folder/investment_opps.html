Copperbelt Province.

1) Lake Kishiba National Monument:

Lake Kishiba is situated near St Anthony's Mission some 26km west of Mpongwe Township at approximately 13o27' south and 27o56' east with an area of 120 hectares. The lake has tremendous wilderness beauty which is accentuated by the surrounding Mushitu forest and the clear bluish hue of the water give it a visual impression of a gigantic natural swimming pool. It is 800 meters in diameter and about 100 meters deep at the edges. the lake is associated with a number of legends and its depth at the center is till unknown which adds to its miystic.

Investment Oppotunities
An upscale tourist resort with at least 80 bed capacity offering international diving exploration - Suitable for water sports, fishing by rod, bird watching, camping, picnicking among other activities.

2) Lake Chilengwa National Monument:

Lake Chilengwa is another one of the sunken lakes in the Copperbelt Province. It lies near the top of the ridge of schist about 16km east of Ndola and about 3km west of the border with the Democratic Republic of Congo. The center-like Lake was formed as a result of the schistose rock collapsing into a large underground cavern in the underlying limestone. The lake is roughly circular with a diameter of 450 meters with a buffer zone of 500 meters around it. The sides of the lake are steeply sloping though there are a few walking trails leading down to the water level. The water level is about 34 meters below the surrounding ground, while the greates depth of the lake is approximated 21 meters. Like Lake Kishiba, the lake is assoiciated with a number of legends and is held in reverence by the local people.

Investment Oppotunities
A medium scale tourist resort offering international diving explorations - suitable for water sports fishing by rod, bird watching, camping, picnicking among other activities.

3) Kapisha Hot Spring Heritage Site:

The Kapisha hot springs are situated in the Kapisha area of Chingola District about 10km from the center of town. They are situated in a grassy and gently slopping plain and are of significant tourism potential as evidence of the fact that albeit the absence of any visitor facilities, the site is already a popular destination for some Chingola residents. the site area covering the hot springs is approximated at 200 meters by 200 meters which would cover the immediate environs of the hot springs and the frontage of the water flo into the Kapisha stream.

Investment Oppotunities
A medium scale tourist resort offering natural heated swimming pools and a small golf course - Also suitable for recreation, camping, picnicking among other activities.

North-Western Province.

Solwezi District:

1) Kifubwa Rockshelter

The site is situated about 5km from Solwezi town center along the kifubwa Stream. The only known site where painted engravings occur in Zambia. These engravings consist almost entirely of inverted 'U' motifs, vertical ines and pock markings. Rock-shelter is located close to a small, but picturesque gorge. The site measures 259HA and the government is constructing a Provincial Cultural Village to diversify the tourism product.

Investment Oppotunities
Lodging/Camping facily, Game ranching and river based activities among others.

2) Mutanda Falls

Mutanda Falls is situated about 30km of Solwezi along the Mutanda river just after the Solwezi-Kasempa road bridge. A series of three successive waterfalls with the smaller two located upstream and downstream of the main falls respectively. The falls are of considerable scenic beauty with a tremendous tourism potential. The area surrounding the water falls has largely retained its natural beauty. At the foot of the main falls is a deep natural pool which is popular with swimmers.

Investment Oppotunities
River based activities such as Kayaking.

3) Mumbezhi Falls

It can be reached by turning south from the Solwezi Mwinilunga road about 120km from Solwezi Town. It is about 7km off this road. Also a series of three successive rivers located where the Mumbezhi river meanders to form an 'island'. The site which is in pristine condition is of great scenic beauty with large pools forming a backdrop to the cascading water.

Investment Oppotunities
Lodging/Camping facily. Adventure activities such as nature walks/trails, canoeing etc.

4) Musonga Falls

Musonga Falls is downstream of the Mutanda Falls about 1km away. It is about 1.5 meters in height and 70-80 meters in width leaving a beautiful appearance to the whole falls. There is also a pleasant pool at the nose of the falls which is ideal for canoeing and swimming for those who are competent at swimmingin running water.

Investment Oppotunities
River based activities such as boating.

Ikelenge District

1) Zambezi Source

The Zambezi Source is located about 20km from the administrative center of Ikelenge and 54km North-West of Mwinilunga Township. It marks the birthplace of Zambia and the source of the Zambezi River.

Investment Oppotunities
Tour operations for packaged visits to sites in the district and lodging facilities in Ikelenge district among others.

Mwinilunga District

1) Nyambwezu Falls

It is situated about 82km from Mwinilunga District on the Nyambwezu River, the falls are bount 20m high. Close to the lip of the falls lies a rock shelter, which is decorated with prehistoric engravings formed mainly of simple lines and pits. Protected site is 36HA.

Investment Oppotunities
Lodging/Camping facily, conference facily, abseiling and white water rafting etc.

2) Lake Chibbesha

Located about 40km off the Mwinilunga H Solwezi road in chief Kanyama's area. With no river inlet, the lake is believed to be underground fed. It is thus a sunken lake formed as a result of seepage dissolving the underground limestone bedrock. It is almost circular in shape and is surrounded by pockets of white beach.

Lodging/Camping, canoeing and regatta, angling, bird viewing, picnicking. Anyting is possible with the white beach. Package tours to lake Chibbesha to include nearby Muzhila falls and Nyang'ombe falls.

Chavuma District

1) Chanda Falls

It is situated approximately 20km from the council offices. It is a series of interconnected waterfalls which manifest a 'stepped' formation. It can only be accessed by crossing the Zambezi River via a pontoon.

Investment Oppotunities
Lodging, boating and other adventure water activities.

2) Chavuma Falls

Located just about 2km from the Council Offices.

Investment Oppotunities
Lodging, boating and other adventure water activities.
